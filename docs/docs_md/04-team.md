# Team Roles

This document explains who is on the RushHour hackathon team and what each person should focus on.

## Team Overview

We are four people:

- Yaw
- Nam
- Jusuf
- Kobi

## Yaw

Primary focus:

- Pitch structure
- Product framing
- MVP direction
- Demo narrative

Goal:

Yaw should make sure the product story is sharp, the MVP proves the core idea, and the demo clearly shows why prediction beats discovery.

## Nam

Primary focus:

- Pitch structure
- MVP build
- Product flow
- User experience

Goal:

Nam should help turn the startup idea into a clear workflow and a working MVP that judges can understand quickly.

## Jusuf

Primary focus:

- Marketing analysis
- Competitor analysis
- Pitch support
- Customer and market scouting

Goal:

Jusuf should clarify the market wedge, map alternatives, gather useful customer and competitor signals, and make the pitch more credible.

## Kobi

Primary focus:

- Marketing analysis
- Competitor analysis
- Pitch support
- Customer and market scouting

Goal:

Kobi should help prove that the problem is real, identify where we can win first, and support the pitch with evidence and sharp positioning.

## Working Principles

- Yaw and Nam own the product story, pitch structure, MVP, and demo flow.
- Jusuf and Kobi own market, competition, scouting, validation, and pitch evidence.
- Everyone should keep the hackathon goal in mind: a specific buyer problem, a working MVP, meaningful AI, and a founder-level pitch.
- When decisions conflict, choose the path that makes the 3-minute pitch and live demo easier to understand.
