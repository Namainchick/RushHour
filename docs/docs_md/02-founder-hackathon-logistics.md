## How The Day Works

After the intro and team formation, the day is mostly self-directed. There are no forced checkpoints and no permission gate before you start building.

- Use the deadline warnings to pace yourself.
- Ask organizers or volunteers for logistics help, not product approval.
- Judges review submissions and pitches in the evening.
- Do not expect daytime investor mentoring or pitch practice from judges during the build phase.
- If you are stuck, use your team, the room, Discord `#help`, and the organizer / help desk team.

## How Judges Decide

Full judging and scoring guide:  
[03-founder-hackathon-judging-scoring-guide.md](03-founder-hackathon-judging-scoring-guide.md)

The spirit is simple: could this become a real company, and did the team execute under pressure?

Judges will look for:

- A painful, specific buyer problem.
- A working demo or credible hosted preview, if submitted.
- A sharp wedge, not a broad platform claim.
- Clear use of AI or modern tooling where it actually improves the product.
- Evidence that someone wants this: user signal, customer conversation, waitlist, pilot interest, usage, or credible domain insight.
- A team that learns quickly and can explain why they are the right people to build it.

You do not need to build the next OpenAI in 12 hours.

You need to ship a working product, a clear pitch, and a reason this could only have been built by you.

To understand exactly how judges think, and how to sharpen your idea, wedge, and pitch to meet that bar, read the Ideas & Strategy guide before June 6. It is the single most useful thing you can do to prepare.
